Frequently Asked Questions

Is Psoriasis Completely Curable?

expand_more
Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition - it cannot always be permanently cured, but symptoms can be very effectively controlled with the right management plan. Most patients with proper treatment experience significantly fewer and milder flare-ups, longer periods of clear skin, and a greatly improved quality of life. Early, consistent management generally leads to better long-term outcomes.

Is Psoriasis Contagious?

expand_more
No. Psoriasis is not contagious in any way. It cannot be spread through touch, sharing clothes, or any form of physical contact. Psoriasis is an autoimmune condition caused by the body's own immune system not an infection.

What Triggers Psoriasis Flare-Ups?

expand_more
Common triggers include stress (one of the most significant), skin injury or sunburn, infections (especially throat infections), certain medications, weather changes, alcohol, and smoking. At Virtue, trigger identification is a core part of every psoriasis management plan helping you understand and reduce your personal triggers for better long-term control.

Can Psoriasis Spread To Other Areas?

expand_more
Psoriasis may spread to new areas of the body if not managed properly. With an appropriate treatment plan, disease progression can be slowed and existing plaques reduced. Identifying and managing triggers is particularly important in preventing the condition from worsening or spreading.

When Will I See Improvement?

expand_more
Many patients notice meaningful symptom relief, reduced itching, less scaling, calmer redness within the first 2-4 weeks of starting treatment. Significant plaque reduction typically becomes visible over 6-12 weeks. Ongoing management with trigger control continues to improve outcomes over months.
