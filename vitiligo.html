Frequently Asked Questions

Is Vitiligo Completely Curable?

expand_more
Vitiligo cannot always be permanently cured, but its progression can often be stabilised with proper treatment. Repigmentation may occur, particularly in early-stage vitiligo, patches near hair follicles, and face or neck areas. Results vary between individuals. Early treatment significantly improves the chances of better outcomes. Our team provides honest, realistic guidance at every step.

Is Vitiligo Contagious?

expand_more
No. Vitiligo is absolutely not contagious in any form. It cannot be spread through touch, sharing clothing, or any form of contact. Vitiligo is an autoimmune condition caused by the body's own immune system affecting melanocytes. You can live, work, and socialise normally without any risk to others.

Can Stress Worsen Vitiligo?

expand_more
Yes, stress is one of the known triggers that may activate or worsen vitiligo in susceptible individuals. Managing stress alongside medical treatment is an important part of the comprehensive management plan at Virtue. Your dermatologist will discuss stress management strategies and lifestyle guidance as part of your care.

When Will I See Results?

expand_more
Stabilisation of spread is typically the first goal and may be seen within weeks of starting treatment. Repigmentation is gradual, it may take several weeks to months and is more likely in early-stage vitiligo and in face and neck patches. Results vary between individuals and patch locations. Your dermatologist will track your progress at every monitoring session.

Is Early Treatment Important?

expand_more
Yes, significantly. Early intervention when vitiligo is still in an active, spreading phase gives the best chance of stabilising the condition before patches enlarge or multiply. Early-stage patches also have a better chance of repigmentation. If you notice new white patches appearing, seeking medical evaluation promptly is strongly recommended.
