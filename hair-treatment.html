FAQs

1. Does PRP really work?

expand_more
Yes. PRP uses growth factors from your own blood to reactivate weakened hair follicles, improve scalp health, and reduce hair fall. It shows best results in early to moderate thinning, with gradual improvement in density over a few sessions.

2. Is it painful?

expand_more
Most patients feel only mild discomfort, like a light pricking or warmth. A numbing agent is applied beforehand to keep the procedure comfortable throughout the 45–60 minute session.

3. When will I see results?

expand_more
Hair fall reduction is usually noticed within 3–6 weeks. Visible improvement in density and new growth typically appears after 2–3 sessions, with best results building over 3–6 months.

4. How many sessions do I need?

expand_more
Most plans include 4–6 initial sessions spaced a few weeks apart, followed by maintenance if needed. The exact number depends on your level of thinning and scalp response.

5. Is it suitable for women?

expand_more
Yes. PRP is safe for both men and women and is commonly used for female pattern thinning, post-partum hair loss, and stress-related shedding with customised protocols.
